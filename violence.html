There is only one problem

The data was acquired by interviewing 151,055 households, which was 242,928 unique persons in the United States. Table 9 estimates 23.2 violent victimizations per 1,000 persons. So it seems like they interviewed 10,471 victims. Those 10,471 victims were randomly selected to represent the whole entire United States. The problem is the data depended on the victim's perception of what race were the perpetrators. In essence, you might have a blame game here.

The data's validity

How could 10,471 victims represent the whole entire United States if there are literally millions of criminals in the justic system? Why not just count real life prison statistics?
