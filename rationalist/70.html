These so-called “Vulcans” expressed their geopolitical objectives through a think-tank, or policy
institute, that they called Project for the New American Century. In one of their publications, a report from
2000 titled, Rebuilding America's Defenses: Strategies, Forces, and Resources for a New Century,
they asserted that the United States should "seek to preserve and extend its position of global leadership"
by "maintaining the preeminence of U.S. military forces”.118 However, they predicted that this would come
about slowly, “absent some catastrophic and catalyzing event, like a new Pearl Harbor.”
