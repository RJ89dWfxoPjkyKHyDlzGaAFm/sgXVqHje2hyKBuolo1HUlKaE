“Scoop and dump” sounds uncomfortably close to a type of robbery tactic known as “smash and
grab”.342 Due to the chaos and destruction of the event, the trail of this wealth is essentially lost.
Unfortunately, it gets worse, as we find evidence of strong-arm robbery tactics. In a November, 2001
article from the New York Times, we read:343
About two weeks ago, a security team spotted scorch marks on a basement
doorway below 4 World Trade Center, on the east side of the ruined complex,
according to officials.
Even in a place of mass devastation and death, those scorch marks got fast attention.
They had not been noticed by a patrol team a few hours earlier, and behind the damaged
-- but intact -- door were nearly a thousand tons of gold and silver. To security officials,
it looked as if someone had tried to break in.
The bank also engaged Kroll Inc., a security business based in New York, to supervise
the relocation of the gold and silver, a process that began this week, The Daily News
reported yesterday.
