304
1. The Messiah would be born in the town of Bethlehem, and his ancestry would be
from the tribe of Judah.
o “But thou, Bethlehem Ephratah, though thou be little among the thousands of
Judah, yet out of thee shall he come forth unto me that is to be ruler in Israel;
whose goings forth have been from of old, from everlasting.” (Micah 5:2)
o “The sceptre shall not depart from Judah, nor a lawgiver from between his feet,
until Shiloh come; and unto him shall the gathering of the people be.” (Genesis
49:10)
2. He would be preceded by a messenger in the wilderness.
o “The voice of him that crieth in the wilderness, Prepare ye the way of the LORD,
make straight in the desert a highway for our God.” (Isaiah 40:3)
o The New Testament fulfillment reads, “In those days came John the Baptist,
preaching in the wilderness of Judaea, and saying, Repent ye: for the kingdom of
heaven is at hand. (Matthew 3:1–2)
3. He would enter Jerusalem on a colt.
o “Rejoice greatly, O daughter of Zion; shout, O daughter of Jerusalem: behold, thy
King cometh unto thee: he is just, and having salvation; lowly, and riding upon an
ass, and upon a colt the foal of an ass.” (Zechariah 9:9)
4. He would be betrayed by a friend.
o “Yea, mine own familiar friend, in whom I trusted, which did eat of my bread, hath
lifted up his heel against me.” (Psalm 41:9)
5. His hands and feet would be pierced.
o “For dogs have compassed me: the assembly of the wicked have inclosed me:
they pierced my hands and my feet.” (Psalm 22:16)
