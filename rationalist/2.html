to find supportive evidence for it, using facts that are corroborative, verifiable, and widely considered to be
mainstream. In pursuit of that effort, this writing provides 785 sources, through which we can attempt to
discern a truthful and sufficiently explanatory worldview. In order to show that the conclusions drawn
throughout this text are not the more rational worldview, one would have to tear down, or discredit, at
least a majority of these sources, and then erect in its place a more sufficiently plausible or explanatory
worldview. Unfortunately, anyone wishing to do so would have their work cut out for them, as the vast
majority of these 785 sources are mainstream, authoritative, and easily verifiable by the public. The
current mainstream view, accepted by the vast majority of people alive today, is the Coincidence theory,
as told by the government and media. However, as we will see, this worldview suffers from irreconcilable
conflictions, unexplained anomalies, staggeringly high probabilistic odds against it, and literal physical
