Lucian of Samosata, The Passing Peregrinus, 2nd century A.D.
Lucian of Samosata was a Greek author who lived in a city on the Euphrates on the outskirts of
the Roman Empire a century after Christ, and frequently wrote about religion. In his book The Passing
Peregrinus, he declared that Jesus was worshiped by his followers and was “The man who was crucified
in Palestine because he introduced this new cult into the world.”439
Lucian’s text is historically significant because it contains one of the earliest evaluations of early
Christianity by a non-Christian author.440
